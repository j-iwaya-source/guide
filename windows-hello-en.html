💡 "Why do I need to enter my password AND set a PIN?"
Windows asks for your password once to verify your identity. After that, the PIN is a short device-specific code used for all future sign-ins. Your PIN is never sent over the internet — it stays on this PC only, making it very secure.
💡 PIN Tips
· Digits only (4 or more) by default
· Check "Include letters and symbols" to allow alphanumeric & special characters
· This PIN is device-specific — it won't work on other PCs
· Be sure to write it down and keep it somewhere safe

Common Issues & Solutions

😔 Face not recognized

"Could not recognize you" appears

✅ Solution

① Check the privacy shutter is open
② Remove mask and try again
③ Check lighting is adequate
④ Use "Improve recognition" to re-register
⑤ If unresolved, sign in with PIN

🔢 Forgot PIN

Cannot remember the PIN

✅ Solution

Click "I forgot my PIN" on the sign-in screen → verify identity with your Microsoft account or domain password → you can then set a new PIN. See the PIN Reset slide for full steps.

⚙️ "Set up" button not shown

"This option is currently unavailable" appears

✅ Solution

Your organization's policy may be restricting this option. Please contact IT via Slack (see support channels below). The IT team will review the policy settings.

🔄 Want to re-register face recognition

Want to reset due to poor accuracy

✅ Solution

Settings → Accounts → Sign-in options → "Facial recognition (Windows Hello)" → "Remove" → then click "Set up" to re-register. Registering with and without glasses improves accuracy.

How to Reset a Forgotten PIN

~3–5 min

Even if you forget your PIN, you can set a new one by verifying your identity with your Microsoft account or domain password.
Your data will not be lost. This process is done from the lock screen, so you can recover without being signed in.

📋 Detailed Steps

1
On the lock screen, click "I forgot my PIN" below the PIN input field.
2
Enter your Microsoft account password (or your organization's domain password) to verify your identity.
3
Enter your new PIN.
Recommended: 6+ digits. Avoid birthdays or sequential numbers.
4
Re-enter the same PIN to confirm and click "OK".
5
Once "Setup complete" appears, you can sign in with your new PIN.

🔢 PIN Strength Guide

⚠️ "I forgot my PIN" is not shown
Your organization's policy may hide this option. In that case, try signing in with face recognition, or contact IT via Slack (see channels below).
💡 Face recognition continues to work after a PIN reset.
Resetting your PIN does not affect your Windows Hello face registration.

❓ Frequently Asked Questions about PIN Reset

Q. Will I lose my data?
No. Resetting your PIN only changes the sign-in method. Your files and applications are not affected.
Q. I also forgot my Microsoft password. What do I do?
Please contact IT via Slack. Use #global_corporate-it if you are at an overseas office, or #help_社内システム if you are at the head office. The IT team will assist with account recovery.
Q. Do I need to re-register face recognition after a PIN reset?
No. Your face recognition settings remain unchanged. A PIN reset does not affect face recognition.
Q. Can I change my PIN multiple times?
Yes, as many times as needed. Go to Settings → Accounts → Sign-in options → PIN (Windows Hello) → "Change" to update it at any time.
