⚠️ Why order matters
Always click "Stop email signing" in GWS before deleting the DNS records.
If done in reverse (DNS deleted first, then GWS stopped), there is a window where GWS is no longer signing but the DNS record still exists — this can briefly cause dkim=fail for outgoing mail. The practical impact is minor, but the correct order is recommended.

📊 Impact of DMARC policy on forwarded mail

Phase Policy Impact on forwarded mail Action required?
Phase 1 (now) p=none ✅ No impact. Forwarded mail is delivered normally. RUA reports reveal the actual state. None (monitor only)
Phase 2 (future) p=quarantine ⚠️ Forwarded mail failing SPF/DKIM may be delivered to the spam folder. Must be addressed during Phase 1
Phase 3 (future) p=reject ❌ Forwarded mail failing SPF/DKIM will be completely blocked (undelivered). Required (see below)

🛠 Recommended mitigations before moving to Phase 2 (for reference)

① SPF forwarding fix — SRS
Configure Sender Rewriting Scheme (SRS) on Postfix. SRS rewrites the Envelope From to the forwarding domain, allowing SPF to pass at the recipient's server.
Can be implemented with the postsrsd package.
② Recipient-side mitigation — ARC
Authenticated Received Chain (ARC)-aware receiving servers (such as Gmail) validate the authentication chain of forwarded mail and may reduce spam classification for legitimate forwards. Gmail already supports ARC.
💡 Recommended action right now
Start by monitoring with p=none and use RUA reports to check how many forwarded messages via Postfix are failing authentication.
If failures are frequent, consider introducing SRS before moving to Phase 2. During the p=none phase, forwarded mail is completely unaffected — no immediate action is required.
